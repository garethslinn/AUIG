Overview

This document introduces the Accessible User Interface Guidelines (AUIG), a comprehensive and easy-to-follow set of robust accessibility guidelines designed for modern user interface design, development, and testing. AUIG is crafted to assist designers, user experience professionals, developers, testers, and other stakeholders in planning, creating, building, and testing accessible user interfaces and components. In today's digital-first world, ensuring that user interfaces are inclusive and accessible to all individuals, regardless of their abilities, is essential. The guidelines outlined in this document provide actionable strategies to develop user interfaces that are not only functional and aesthetically pleasing but also accessible to users with diverse needs. By adopting these standards, teams can enhance usability, foster inclusivity, and comply with evolving accessibility regulations, all through a structured and intuitive approach that makes implementing accessibility straightforward and effective.

This guide is tailored for:

  • Developers: Building user interfaces that prioritise accessibility.
  • Designers: Crafting visually and functionally inclusive interfaces.
  • UX Professionals: Understanding and addressing the needs of diverse user groups.
  • Testers: Ensuring compliance with accessibility standards during quality assurance.
  • Product Owners and Managers: Integrating accessibility principles into project goals and workflows.

AUIG serves as a vital resource for anyone involved in the user interface development lifecycle, providing a modern and structured approach that is intuitive to understand and easy to use. By following these guidelines, organisations can ensure their digital products are inclusive, user-friendly, and aligned with best practices in accessibility, ultimately enhancing the overall user experience for everyone.
